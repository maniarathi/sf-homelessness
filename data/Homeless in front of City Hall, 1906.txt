  Homeless in front of City Hall, 1906


    City Hall has been a magnet for homeless encampments for more than
    100 years

Image courtesy of 1906 San Francisco Earthquake and Fire Digital
Collection
<http://oac.cdlib.org/findaid/ark:/13030/hb8779p2cx&brand=eqf>,
California Historical Society <http://www.californiahistoricalsociety.org/>

The devastation of the 1906 San Francisco Earthquake created a homeless
population of around 250,000 people. Refugees set up makeshift camps all
around the city. This photograph is captioned: “Dwellings of the
homeless — shelter built of wreckage, near ruined City Hall, San
Francisco, Cal.”
