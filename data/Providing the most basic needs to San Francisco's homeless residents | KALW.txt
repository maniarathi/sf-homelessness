Meeting personal hygiene needs are hard when you're living on the
street. But a new nonprofit is trying to change that. Simply the Basics
launched just last year and is providing homeless shelters and
nonprofits with basic hygienic needs like tampons- but also soap,
toothpaste, deodorant and other personal care items that help keep
people healthy, but also help them live with dignity. It’s called the
Hygiene Bank. Meghan Freebeck is founder of Simply the Basics. She spoke
with KALW's Hana Baba.

Learn more about Simply the Basicshere <http://www.simplythebasics.org/>.
