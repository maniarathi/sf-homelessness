  On the June 29th edition of Your Call, we’ll continue our week long
series on San Francisco’s homeless crisis by talking about how we got
here and real solutions.

The city’s last official one-day count in 2015 put the homeless
population at 6,686, but advocate say the number is much higher. Every
year, San Francisco spends $240 million on homelessness, but the problem
is only getting worse. What are real solutions? It’s Your Call, with
Rose Aguilar, and you.

*Guests:*

Paul Boden, executive director of the Western Regional Advocacy Project

Jennifer Friedenbach, executive director of the Coalition on Homelessness
