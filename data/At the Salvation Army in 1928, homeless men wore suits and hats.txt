  At the Salvation Army in 1928, homeless men wore suits and hats


    Splitting kindling in SoMA

Written on the back of this image: “Here are homeless, jobless,
destitute men splitting kindling at the Salvation Army woodyard, a
Community Chest agency, at 864–868 Harrison street. They receive meals
at the soup kitchen and beds in an adjoining dormitory. When the
dormitory capacity is reached, they welcome shelter under or on top of
benches and even on the concrete floors.”

/Photo Credit: //San Francisco History Center, San Francisco Public
Library/ <http://sflib1.sfpl.org:82/record=b1039240>/./
